# In my words

Hi there :wave:

I am a programmer-coach (like a player-coach in sport) with over a decade of experience building software teams and products.

I started my career programming at Careem which went on to be the first unicorn out of the Middle East. Besides writing bucket loads of code in various domains (locations, payments, matching etc.), I was exposed to a wide array of tools and technologies and learnt how to scale architecture; from one server when I joined to thousands as years wore on. I also met some fantastic people from whom I learnt my engineering chops.

Along the way I realised that throwing technologies (or people) wasn't always the most effective way of solving problems. Pains were alleviated sometimes, but rarely solved. All the complexity we were piling on - the layers of code, tools, frameworks and people - looked marvellous to the eye, but was it the most effective solution to our problems?

I wondered if there was a better way.

Around this time, I was introduced to a whole new paradigm of thinking in the form of Lean, Systems Thinking, Theory of Constraints, and of course, Extreme Programming (XP). Thanks in large part to a colleague I crossed paths with, [Dragan](https://dragan-stepanovic.github.io/), from whom I also learnt the value of a great coach.

I have since been trying to learn and apply these new ideas (new for me at least; the ideas themselves are lent from many brilliant thinkers of the past, like Taiichi and Deming) to make work not only better but also joyful for myself and those around me.
